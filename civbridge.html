Matboard Bridge
November 2023
GitHub

Using the concepts taught in CIV102, three others and I designed and created a bridge out of Matboard (cardboard-like material) that supported a small train initially weighing 400N. The train ran across the bridge multiple times, each time increasing in weight. It was calculated the bridge could withstand 540N of force before failure. The bridge failed at 500N passing the first two test cases of 400N and 447N. The image shows the train being pulled across our bridge. The bridge was made of pieces of matboard attached together with cement glue. The image above to the right was taken while building the bridge.

We went through several iterations of the cross-section (left above) and ran code simulations in Python to test each one. Our final cross-section (right above) was a double webbed I-beam cross-section with a double-layered top flange and a single bottom flange. The bridge had 12 diaphragms evenly spaced between it. This project let me appreciate the iterative process of design as we would create a cross-section, run the simulation for it, and then change it to improve it. We did this multiple times until we landed on our current cross-section. The simulations can be found here: Repository

CNC Router
October - December 2024

Two others and I designed this CNC Router and I CADed it in SOLIDWORKS. The Router was created as a final course project for MIE243: Engineering Design. The router has 3 axes, uses a direct drive spindle and is supported across the y-axis. The x and y axes use ball screws with stepper motors and linear rails for support. The z-axis uses a lead screw with a stepper motor. It uses an air coolant and uses a standard T-slot table. The purpose of the project was to design a router that could be used by the hobbyist community

First my group identified what hobbyists are looking for in a CNC: affordability ($2000-$5000), ease of assembly, and capability of handling wood, plastics, and aluminum. It should also feature components that are easy to source or fabricate and allow for upgrades. Next, we created Engineering Specifications of what we needed to have in our design. This was our list of engineering specifications:

We created a design toolkit of possible mechanisms we could use in a router (design toolkit shown below). After the formation of the toolkit, we created several candidate designs. We analyzed our candidate designs and selected the best one. We selected a direct-driven spindle for better speed and torque. Stepper motors for affordability and ease of use. Flat profile rails for stability and precision. We chose ball screws for X and Y axes and lead screws for Z-axis for accuracy and cost-effectiveness.

We created the frame of the CNC out of sheet metal, but we sourced the motors, lead screw, ball screws, linear rails, and the direct drive spindle. We created a bill of materials and did a cost analysis. Our CNC costs $3392.02 which is in our proposed budget of $2000 to $5000.

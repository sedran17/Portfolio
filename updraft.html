Updraft
September 2024 - Present

In September 2024, I joined the University of Toronto Robotics Association’s Combat Team (UTRA Combat), which designs and builds robots in the 1lb, 3lb, and 30lb weight classes for competitions across Canada and the United States. I am a member of the two-person team developing Updraft, a 3lb beater-bar robot pictured above. My contributions have focused on the protection of Updraft's electronic components and Updraft's adaptability.

One of the challenges our team faced was that Updraft’s spinning motors occasionally damaged its battery during matches. To address this, I designed a protective shield for the battery, as well as a secondary shield to protect the receiver from contact with the weapon.

An important aspect of combat robotics is preparing for a wide variety of opponents. For example, Updraft, a vertical beater-bar, regularly encounters other designs, such as horizontal spinners whose weapons spin parallel to the ground. To protect Updraft against horizontal robots, I designed a titanium wedge that prevents horizontal weapons from striking the front of Updraft. The wedge was modeled using SOLIDWORKS’ sheet metal tools and then manufactured in the University of Toronto’s machine shop.

Captain Weaver
January - April 2024

Basket weaving is the process by which baskets are created from reeds or cordage. Cordage (below left) is wrapped around reeds in repetitive motions to weave a basket. Basket-weaving is an activity enjoyed by many elderly members of the Toronto Spinners and Weavers Guild who suffer from arthritis, an inflammatory joint condition. This project was created for ESC102: Praxis II in a team of four people. We created a basket-weaving aid for members of the Guild who suffer from arthritis.

“Captain Weaver” is an aid for the beginning part of basket weaving. In the handheld mode, the reed or cordage is woven around the needle on top of our prototype to create the base. As the user is weaving they can rotate the needle and the base of the basket by using their thumb to push the “pirate ship wheel”. This allows the user to cut down on the harmful pinching motion needed for basket weaving. My team and I designed Captain Weaver, and I CADed it using Autodesk Fusion 360.
